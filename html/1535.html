Dear Rovers,

Since the start of Version 1.1 CBT, our work has been overshadowed by unauthorized content leaks. These leaks have spread fast and wide, including story cutscenes, storyboard drafts, character concept art, and even discarded environment assets. Many of you have expressed frustration at encountering leaked content despite your efforts to avoid it, largely due to platform algorithms. These unverified leaks have dulled the impact of our official version updates and caused major spoilers, undermining the experience we've carefully crafted for you.

Last month, we issued a statement condemning leaks and received widespread support from our community. Thanks to this, many leakers realized the harm they were causing and ceased their activities. Unfortunately, with the launch of the Version 1.4 CBT server, we are once again facing severe leaks of unreleased content.

Developing a new version is meticulous and time-consuming. Character creation, for example, begins several versions ahead with user research, followed by months of design, development, testing, and fine-tuning. Before each update, we conduct a closed beta test to ensure the final product meets player expectations. Promotion only begins when everything is ready, with methodically scheduled moments to provide the best experience. We believe this approach ensures consistent, polished releases, allowing players and content creators to fully engage with each update. Unfortunately, leaks disrupt this process, taking away the element of surprise and undermining the hard work of our team members who put their hearts into every detail.

In response to the damage caused by these leaks, we have implemented stronger measures to prevent future incidents. These measures include reinforcing confidentiality in our test servers, issuing formal warnings, and collaborating with various content-sharing platforms to remove unauthorized material. Additionally, we have initiated legal proceedings against severe breaches of intellectual property, which are currently progressing through the courts. We are committed to defending our work against unauthorized distribution and are prepared to take further legal actions as needed.

Once again, we extend our deepest thanks to all of you for your love and support.
